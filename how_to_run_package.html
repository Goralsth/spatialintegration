Parkinson’s disease (PD) is a progressive neurodegenerative disease characterized by the presence of intraneuronal aggregates of misfolded ɑ-synuclein, called Lewy pathology. Using GeoMx, a bulk spatial transcriptomics technology, we recently identified a molecular signature associated with cortical Lewy pathology, Lewy Associated Molecular dysfunction from aggregates (LAMDA) that is conserved across PD patient brains and mouse models of PD1. This signature denotes alterations in DNA repair, mitochondrial, synaptic pathways etc. These changes were resolved within the same brain utilizing bulk spatial transcriptomics technology (GeoMx). Preceding transcriptomic profiling of Parkinson’s disease involved comparing transcriptomic profiles of healthy with PD (or more broadly Lewy body diseases) brains, without resolution on aggregate status but at bulk and single-cell/nuclei levels (Sn)2–5. The degree to which these alterations overlapped with the LAMDA signature is unclear. In our preliminary data, we utilized previously published Sn data comparing healthy controls to Lewy body diseased (LBD) brains2 to compare to our spatial data of neurons from healthy controls, non-aggregate (Agg-) bearing neurons and aggregate bearing (Agg+) neurons from PD brains. We found that differences between healthy neurons (healthy) to Agg- bearing neurons were positively correlated with the differences between healthy to LBD brains (r = 0.20 , p = 4.04e−73). In addition, both the differences between Agg+ and healthy (r=-0.07,p<0.001), and the Agg+ and Agg- neurons (r=-0.17,p<0.001) were negatively correlated with the differences between healthy to LBD brains. Though weakly correlated, this data highlights alterations induced by Lewy pathology were not understood prior to the LAMDA signature. The LAMDA signature, however, is derived from bulk-spatial transcriptomics data and current single-cell spatial technologies lack the depth necessary to probe molecular alterations at a genome wide level. Eigengenes have been utilized in to attempt label transfer in similar data previously but lack evidence for diagonal integration and arbitrarily transfer labels, limiting the scope of downstream analysis6. Presently, there is not a method that combines diagonal integration and transfer of the “spatial” label to the unlabeled Sn data. Our approach aims to resolve technical limitations that prevent labeled single-nuclei data for aggregate status. We hypothesize that non-negative matrix factorization and/or the modified causal transformer decoder foundation model scGPT6 can learn shared molecular alterations in lower dimensional integrated space between these two data types and successfully transfer “spatial” labels across the diagonal to the Sn data.
